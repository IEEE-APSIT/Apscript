About

IEEE Apsit SB is hosting ApScript Hackathon on 6th & 7th February 2020, a programming endeavor, drawing crowds from all over the country giving an opportunity to bright minds of our nation to prove their potential and win exciting prizes. IEEE APSIT Student Branch comes under IEEE Bombay Section and is recognized to be one the most proactive student branch and has been acclaimed as the upcoming branch with the most potential. Our aim is to help students achieve a successful career by providing the best opportunities and making them eligible for current world technology. Founded in 1963, IEEE is the largest technical professional organization in the world with more than 423,000 members from over 160 countries! IEEE is at the forefront of undertaking numerous initiatives worldwide. IEEE is the trusted voice for engineering, computing, and information technology around the globe. It aims to promote science and development in Electrical, Electronics, Computer Science, and related disciplines.

Rounds

This event consists of three rounds.

  • Inaugration Ceremony

    5th February, 5.30 PM

    • Experienced speakers and judges will be introduced.
    • Details of the hackathon will be discussed.
    • The session will take place on YouTube Live.
    • Links to Problem Statements will be released only after the session in chats so everyone has to attend this session.

  • Round 1

    6th February, 12.00 AM to 5.00 PM

    • Make a 2 min video max and upload it to a drive link we will provide. You need to show a ppt in this video explaining your proposed solution to the problem, tech stack, how you will implement it, etc.
    • They do not need to show a working model in this round but a working model will account for more points.
    • The top 50% of teams will be selected from each track.
    • No submissions will be accepted after 5.00 PM. If you fail to submit the presentation before 5.00 PM you will automatically be eliminated.
    • Results of Round 1 will be declared by 7th February, 9.00 AM. All participants will continue building their solution until then after they have submitted their presentation on 6th February

  • Round 2

    7th February, 11.00 AM to 3.00 PM

    • Google Meet with judges domain-wise. Every team will privately interact with judges one team at a time.
    • Participants will show their progress to the judges and points will be allotted based on the actual work done.
    • JJudges will give suggestions to improve if necessary.
    • Results of Round 2 will be declared by 4.00 PM.
    • The top 10 teams will be selected for Round 3 from each domain.

  • Round 3

    7th February, till 11.59 PM

    • The top 10 teams from each domain will work on improvements suggested by judges in Round 2 and try to complete as much project as possible.
    • No submissions will be accepted after 11.59 PM
    • If any team fails to submit their final solution with the working model of the project before the deadline they will automatically be eliminated
    • Judges will analyze all submissions throughout next week and final results will be announced on the 13th of February at a prize distribution ceremony. All top 10 ten teams from each domain will have to be present as well as any anyone else is welcome to join

FAQ

A hackathon is a social coding event where programmers, designers, and developers collaborate to solve a problem and compete for cash prizes. It’s one part party, one part work-your-butt-off overnight battle against the clock and the competition.

People participate in hackathons for lots of reasons: the challenge, the creative outlet, the community collaboration, the networking, the swag…

This event is open to all students and young professionals from all over the world.

While experience coding and programming is a huge plus, teams will also need people with strong presentation skills and brilliant ideas.

You are encouraged to participate in the Hackathon Training Workshops. Topics, dates, and times will be announced at a later date. But other than that you are expected to learn on your own.
